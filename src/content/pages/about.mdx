The name comes from an unlikely pairing that felt, in the end, exactly right. Logic — the attempt to think clearly, to follow evidence, to resist the comfort of convenient conclusions. Luminescence — light that comes from within, not reflected from an external source. Put together: the project of thinking clearly from the inside out.

That's what this site is trying to be.

## Where this comes from

I'm not an academic or a policy expert. I'm a person trying to understand what is happening and what it means — for myself, and for everyone navigating the same moment.

I've spent time with contemplative practice — with what it means to examine your own mind seriously, to cultivate attention, to understand your own values not as things you inherited but as things you can consciously choose. And I've spent time watching the trajectory of AI and autonomous systems, becoming increasingly convinced that this technology is not a productivity story. It is a civilisational story, with all the disruption and danger that implies.

What struck me was that no one seemed to be holding both at once. The contemplative tradition has profound things to say about navigating uncertainty and change — but tends to stay quiet about political economy. The technology analysts are often sharp about capability and risk — but rarely venture into what it means for inner life, identity, or what it takes to remain a free person in a concentrating world. And the political commentary is usually too tribal to be useful.

Logical Luminescence is an attempt to hold all of it, honestly, from a perspective that is personal rather than authoritative. I don't have the answers. I have questions I think are worth asking, a commitment to following where they lead, and a belief that other people are asking the same things.

## A note on approach

This site takes seriously the possibility that things could go badly — that concentrated AI power, disrupted economies, and strained political institutions are a genuinely dangerous combination. It doesn't think despair is useful. It also doesn't think optimism is required.

What it does think is that clarity is possible, that inner resilience can be cultivated, and that understanding the world you're actually in — rather than a more comfortable version of it — is the beginning of meaningful response.

The goal isn't to frighten. It's to help you think — and through thinking, to find your footing.

## On working with AI

There's an honesty I should name directly: this site is itself partly produced in collaboration with AI tools, including Claude. I find that fact worth acknowledging rather than obscuring. The thinking is mine. The research, the framing, the editorial choices — these come from a human perspective. But I use AI as a genuine thinking partner, and part of what this project explores is precisely what that kind of collaboration looks and feels like when done with intention.

That seems worth being transparent about on a site that takes the human-AI relationship seriously.
