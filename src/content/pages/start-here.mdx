Something is changing. You already know this.

Not just the technology. The technology is almost the easy part to describe. What's harder to name — and what most conversations about AI quietly avoid — is what it means for how we live, who holds power, and what becomes of ordinary people in the middle of a disruption this large.

This site exists because I found myself unable to locate those conversations. There's plenty written about AI's capabilities and its risks. There's very little written honestly about the human experience of living through this — the uncertainty, the political anxiety, the question of how to remain meaningfully yourself when the ground is moving.

## What I think is happening

I want to be direct about this. Artificial intelligence and autonomous robotics are not simply productivity tools. They represent a shift in the economic foundations of society comparable to — and probably exceeding — the industrial revolution. That kind of shift doesn't just change what people do for work. It destabilises institutions, concentrates wealth and power, and creates conditions in which existing political structures come under enormous strain.

History suggests that when those structures fail to manage that strain, the response is rarely more freedom. It tends, instead, toward control.

That is not a partisan observation. It is a structural one. And I think it deserves to be named plainly, because understanding it changes what "adapting to AI" actually means.

## What you'll find here

This site is organised around four connected ideas.

The first is that **the inner life matters more, not less, in this moment** — that contemplative practice, self-knowledge, and values clarity are not retreats from the world but navigation tools within it.

The second is that **honest, non-hyped assessment of the technology itself is necessary** — what it actually does, what it can't, and what its proliferation means in practice.

The third is that **the political and structural stakes need to be part of this conversation**, not bracketed off as too difficult or too divisive.

The fourth is that **adaptation is possible** — that individuals and communities can find genuine agency and flourishing within the transition, if they approach it with their eyes open.

These aren't separate topics. They are a single argument, built from the inside out.

## Who this is for

This site isn't for people who have already decided. It's for people who are still working it out — who find both the AI-utopian and the AI-doomer framings unsatisfying, who suspect the truth is more complicated and more personal than either, and who want a space to think seriously without being lectured at.

You don't need a particular politics to be here. You do need a willingness to hold difficult questions without demanding quick answers. And probably some tolerance for the possibility that your relationship to technology, to power, and to your own inner life are all connected — and that examining one will inevitably implicate the others.

If that sounds like where you are, you're in the right place.
