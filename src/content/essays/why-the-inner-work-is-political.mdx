There is a version of self-improvement that is deeply, structurally apolitical. It locates all problems in the individual — in their habits, their mindset, their discipline — and treats the social and economic conditions those individuals inhabit as essentially fixed background. This version is, at the moment, extremely popular. And it is, I think, exactly wrong for this historical moment.

The version I want to argue for is different. It holds that inner development — genuine self-knowledge, the cultivation of attention, the slow work of understanding your own values — is not a retreat from the world. It is preparation for it. And in a world being reorganised by artificial intelligence, with all the political consequences that reorganisation implies, that preparation is not a luxury. It is a form of resistance.
